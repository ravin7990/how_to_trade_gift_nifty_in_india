How to trade Oil in India

Basics for trading crude oil instruments — contract types, drivers of price, and practical risk management for Indian traders.

Overview

Crude oil is a globally traded commodity with high volatility. Traders in India can access oil via futures (WTI/Brent), ETFs, CFDs, or commodity derivatives where available.

Key drivers

  • Global supply/demand, OPEC decisions and geopolitics.
  • US inventory reports (API/EIA) and macroeconomic indicators.
  • Currency effects when trading in USD instruments from INR accounts.

Risk management

  • Use position sizing and strict stops — oil can gap on news.
  • Consider hedging strategies if you have exposure in business operations.
