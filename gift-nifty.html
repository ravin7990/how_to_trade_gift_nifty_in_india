What is Gift Nifty?

Gift Nifty is a futures contract based on India’s benchmark index — the NIFTY 50. It is traded on the NSE International Exchange (NSE IFSC) located in GIFT City (Gujarat International Finance Tec-City).

Earlier, this contract was widely known as SGX Nifty because it was traded on the Singapore Exchange (SGX). In July 2023 the contract shifted from Singapore to India’s GIFT City and was renamed Gift Nifty. The move helped bring offshore Nifty trading under Indian regulation and strengthened GIFT City as a global financial hub.

History of Gift Nifty

Before 2023, international investors who wanted exposure to Indian markets commonly traded the SGX Nifty in Singapore. SGX Nifty became popular because it allowed global participation, provided longer trading hours, and reflected global market sentiment.

To increase domestic control and deepen liquidity inside India, the NSE shifted the contract to NSE IFSC in GIFT City. From July 3, 2023, SGX Nifty officially became Gift Nifty — a notable milestone in India’s financial development strategy.

Why Gift Nifty is Important for Indian Traders

Gift Nifty trades for extended hours (roughly 21 hours a day across global sessions). Because of these extended hours, it often reflects:

  • US market movement
  • European market trends
  • Other Asian market sentiment
  • Reactions to global news and economic events

For example, if Nifty closed at 22,000 and Gift Nifty is trading at 22,150 before Indian markets open, that usually signals a gap-up opening. That is why traders often check Gift Nifty early in the morning before planning Nifty futures or options trades.

Basic Strategy for Beginners

If you are new, follow a structured approach:

  • Check US market closing trend and major global indices.
  • Observe Asian markets and Gift Nifty in the morning pre-open session.
  • Compare Gift Nifty price with the previous Nifty close to gauge expected opening gaps.
  • Plan your trade with proper risk management: position sizing, stop-losses, and predefined exit rules.
  • Start small and focus on capital preservation — avoid overtrading.

Risks Involved in Gift Nifty Trading

Gift Nifty is a futures contract and typically involves leverage. This means profits can multiply quickly, but losses can also increase rapidly. Major risks include:

  • High volatility and fast intraday moves.
  • Global macro events that drive large gaps.
  • Margin requirements that may change with market conditions.
  • Overnight price gaps when Indian markets are closed.

Never trade without a clear risk-management plan. Consider using defined-risk strategies or smaller position sizes if you are learning.
