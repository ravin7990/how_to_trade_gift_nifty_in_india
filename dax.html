How to trade DAX in India

A practical guide for Indian traders to access the German DAX index — instruments, timing, and risk management.

Overview

The DAX represents major German equities. Indian traders can gain exposure using futures, ETFs listed internationally, or CFDs via brokers that offer European instruments.

Key considerations

  • Trading hours: know the Frankfurt session and overlaps with India time zone.
  • Product choice: choose between futures, ETFs and CFDs based on cost and availability.
  • Taxes & regulations: check local tax implications and any restrictions on international trading.
  • News sensitivity: DAX is sensitive to Eurozone economic data and central bank moves.

Practical tips

  • Monitor European session open/close for liquidity spikes.
  • Use proper stop management — overnight gaps can be significant.
  • Consider currency impact if your account base is INR.
